SHAH RUKH KHAN

Shah Rukh Khan, often referred to as the “King of Bollywood,” is a renowned Indian actor, film producer, and television personality. Born on November 2, 1965, in New Delhi, India, Shah Rukh Khan has become one of the most influential and successful actors in the Indian film industry.

Shah Rukh Khan's journey to stardom was not an easy one. He grew up in a middle-class family, and his father ran a transport company. After completing his schooling at St. Columba's School in Delhi, Shah Rukh Khan pursued his higher education at Hansraj College, where he earned his Bachelor's degree in Economics. Later, he enrolled at the Jamia Millia Islamia University to pursue a Master's degree in Mass Communication, but he decided to drop out to pursue a career in acting.

Shah Rukh Khan began his acting career in the late 1980s with appearances in several television series, including “Fauji” (1988) and “Circus” (1989). However, his breakthrough came with the television series “Dil Dariya” (1988), where he played the lead role. His charming persona and natural acting talent caught the attention of filmmakers, leading him to make his film debut in the critically acclaimed movie “Deewana” in 1992. The film's success established him as a rising star in Bollywood.

In the years that followed, Shah Rukh Khan starred in a series of commercially successful films, such as “Baazigar” (1993), “Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge” (1995), “Dil To Pagal Hai” (1997), and “Kuch Kuch Hota Hai” (1998). These movies solidified his position as one of the most bankable actors in the industry and earned him a massive fan following not only in India but also across the globe.

Apart from his acting career, Shah Rukh Khan is also a successful film producer. In 1999, he founded his production company, Red Chillies Entertainment, which has produced several hit films, including “Main Hoon Na” (2004), “Om Shanti Om” (2007), “Chennai Express” (2013), and “Raees” (2017). He has also ventured into other business ventures, including owning the Kolkata Knight Riders, a team in the Indian Premier League (IPL).

Referred to in the media as the "Baadshah of Bollywood" and "King Khan", he has appeared in over 100 films, and earned numerous accolades, including 14 Filmfare Awards.

Awards and Recognitions

Khan is one of the most decorated Bollywood actors. He has received 14 Filmfare Awards from 30 nominations and special awards, including winning eight for Best Actor; he is tied for the most in this category with Dilip Kumar. Khan has won the Filmfare Award for Best Actor for his performances in Baazigar (1993), Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge (1995), Dil To Pagal Hai (1997), Kuch Kuch Hota Hai (1998), Devdas (2002), Swades (2004), Chak De! India (2007) and My Name Is Khan (2010).

Although he has never won a National Film Award, he was awarded the Padma Shri by the Government of India in 2005. The Government of France has awarded him both the Order of Arts and Letters (2007), and the fifth degree of the French Legion of Honour, the Chevalier Légion d'honneur (2014). Khan has received five honorary doctorates; the first from The University of Bedfordshire in 2009, the second from The University of Edinburgh in 2015, the third from Maulana Azad National Urdu University in 2016, and his latest from The University of Law and La Trobe University in 2019.
